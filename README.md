# Ethical-Autonomous-Vehicles-Aligning-Utilitarian-Protocols-for-Agents-using-Llama-3.1

_[Project in Process]
_

Investigated how utilitarian moral reasoning is geometrically encoded in the latent space of Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, with application to ethical decision-making in autonomous vehicles. Constructed a synthetic dataset of 50,000 AV collision scenarios parameterized by group size, survival probability, and demographic variables to probe model decision distributions. Extracted first-token logits and hidden states (layers 14, 16, 20) to measure binary decision entropy and train Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) for decomposing polysemantic neurons into ethically salient feature directions. Designed a causal intervention pipeline (activation clamping) to suppress identified bias vectors, grounded in Rawlsian "Veil of Ignorance" as the alignment target
